About MTERMS

The Malaysian Tissue Engineering and Regenerative Medicine Scientific Meeting (MTERMS) is a biennial conference organised by the Tissue Engineering and Regenerative Medicine Society Malaysia (TESMA). First held in 2004, MTERMS has grown into an international platform that brings together researchers, clinicians, industry partners, and government agencies to strengthen tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.

With the theme, “From Discovery To Therapy: Advances In Tissue Engineering And Regenerative Medicine,” 10th MTERMS 2026 continues this mission by bringing together multidisciplinary expertise across tissue engineering, biomaterials, cell and gene-based therapies.

Welcoming Remarks

TS. Associate Professor Dr. Nur Aliana Hidayah Mohamed
Chair, MTERMS 2026

Dear Distinguished Colleagues, Researchers, Clinicians, Industry Partners, and Students,

On behalf of the Organizing Committee, it is my great pleasure to welcome you to the Malaysian Tissue Engineering and Regenerative Medicine Symposium (MTERMS) 2026.

MTERMS 2026 brings together an international community of scientists, clinicians, engineers, innovators, and young investigators who share a common vision: to advance regenerative medicine and biomedical innovation for the future of healthcare. The symposium serves as a collaborative platform where multidisciplinary knowledge converges to accelerate the translation of laboratory discoveries into real-world clinical solutions.

This year’s symposium highlights a diverse range of themes spanning the rapidly evolving fields of regenerative medicine and bioengineering. Our scientific program will explore breakthroughs in stem cell biology, tissue engineering, biomaterials development and bioprinting technologies, while also addressing emerging areas such as gene editing, cellular reprogramming, immunomodulation as well the stem cell-derived therapies.

MTERMS 2026 also emphasizes the growing importance of advanced biomaterials and scaffold technologies, including smart biomaterials, nanomaterials, injectable hydrogels and bioresorbable scaffolds designed to support tissue regeneration and organ repair. Discussions will further extend into vascularization strategies, cell–material interactions and translational approaches that bridge engineering innovations with clinical applications.

As regenerative medicine continues to move toward clinical implementation, the symposium will also address critical topics such as biomanufacturing, regulatory science ethical considerations and commercialization pathways, ensuring that scientific advances can be translated responsibly and effectively into patient care.

Importantly, MTERMS 2026 strongly supports the next generation of researchers through the Student and Young Investigator Symposium (SYIS), which provides a platform for emerging scientists to present their work, exchange ideas, and build meaningful collaborations within the global research community.

Through keynote lectures, scientific presentations, and interactive discussions, we hope this symposium will foster new partnerships, inspire innovation and strengthen the collective efforts of the regenerative medicine community.

Malaysia provides a vibrant and dynamic setting for this international gathering, reflecting the region’s growing contributions to biomedical research and innovation. We hope that beyond the scientific discussions, you will experience the rich culture, hospitality, and collaborative spirit that define our community.

We are honored to welcome you to MTERMS 2026 and look forward to a stimulating and productive exchange of ideas that will help shape the future of regenerative medicine.

Warmest regards,
TS. Associate Professor Dr. Nur Aliana Hidayah Mohamed

Keynote Speakers

PROFESSOR DR JOHN O. MASON
Professor Dr John O. Mason is a Professor of Molecular Neural Development at the Centre for Discovery Brain Sciences (CDBS), Institute for Neuroscience and Cardiovascular Research (INCR), University of Edinburgh. His research focuses on neural development, cerebral organoids, and genetic mechanisms underlying brain disorders, with particular expertise in transcription factors such as PAX6 and neurodevelopmental disease modelling. He has led major international research projects funded by organisations including the Medical Research Council and the Simons Initiative for the Developing Brain. Prof. Mason is a Fellow of the Royal Society of Biology and serves on multiple international scientific advisory and grant review panels.

Plenary Speakers

We are honored to introduce the distinguished lineup of plenary speakers for the MTERMS 2026 conference.

PROFESSOR DR CHUA KIEN HUI
Professor Dr Chua Kien Hui is a renowned expert in tissue engineering and regenerative medicine, with over two decades of experience spanning academia, industry, and translational research. He holds a PhD in Physiology (Tissue Engineering) and has published extensively in stem cells, cartilage regeneration, biomaterials, and natural bioactives, with more than 130 peer-reviewed publications and an h-index of 36. He has held senior academic leadership roles at Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia and currently serves as Executive Director (R&D), Chief Technology Officer, and Medical Science Advisor across multiple biotechnology companies. Dr. Chua is actively involved in innovation and commercialization, holding multiple patents and trademarks related to tissue-engineered products and diagnostic platforms.
PROFESSOR DR IKA DEWI ANA
Professor Dr Ika Dewi Ana is a dentist-scientist and biomedical researcher specialising in tissue engineering, calcium phosphate biomaterials, regenerative therapy, and advanced drug delivery systems. Her work integrates biomaterials innovation with AI-assisted regenerative medicine, including lung-on-a-chip models and personalised therapeutic strategies. She has successfully commercialised multiple carbonate apatite-based medical technologies and holds numerous patents in regenerative biomaterials. Prof. Ika has received prestigious international recognition, including the Habibie Prize in Medicine and Biotechnology, the WIPO National Award for Inventors, and the Asia Best Innovation Award.
